FirFir

Also known as fitfit, this dish is made of small pieces of shredded taita that are mixed with lots of
sauce from a leftover tsebhi. The taita and the sauce are stir-fried together so that the bread absorbs the
sauce and softens. It is typically eaten for breakfast as a way to use.

Ingredients

  • 1 injera torn into pieces
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • 40 grams (⅓ cup) diced purple onions
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • ⅓ cup water
  • 2 tablespoons dry white wine
  • 1 tablespoon + 1 teaspoon berbere paste
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 10-15 very soft dates, seeded and halved or cut
  • Green jalapeño chilli, sliced thinly

Recipe

  1. Heat a medium sized saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions and salt to sweat for about five minutes, until they become translucent.
  3. Add the oil and garlic, cooking for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl whisk together the water, wine, berbere, and tomato paste
  5. Tip the liquid into the pan and stir to combine with the onions and garlic.
  6. Add the dates and cook for another minute.
  7. Carefully stir the injera pieces through the sauce, trying not to break them up as you stir.
  8. Tip onto a plate and top with the jalapeño slivers.
